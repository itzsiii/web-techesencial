Why does paper make us smarter?

Jan 10, 2026 â€¢ 4 min read

In the era of tablets and AI, it might seem that handwriting is a thing of the past. Neuroscience says otherwise.

The Analog Brain

Recent studies show that the physical movement of handwriting activates the brain's reticular activating system. Basically: you tell your mind "this is important". When typing, the movement is identical for all letters; when writing, each stroke is unique.

Retention vs. Speed

When typing, we transcribe quickly but without processing. When writing by hand, we are slower, which forces us to synthesize and understand the information before putting it down.
