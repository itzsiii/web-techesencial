Decision Fatigue When Buying Tech

Have you ever spent 3 hours comparing two mice that cost the same and do the same thing? That is analysis paralysis.

The Paradox of Choice

Psychologist Barry Schwartz explained it perfectly: The more options we have, the less satisfied we are with our choice. We always think "what if the other one was better?". The human brain is not designed to choose between 500 types of USB cables.

How I Choose (and help you)

At Tech Essentials, my job is to reduce your options. Instead of giving you a list of "The 50 best keyboards", I give you the best value for money and the best premium one. Simplifying is the key to happiness in modern shopping.

Next time you hesitate, ask yourself: "Will this decision matter in 5 years?". If the answer is no, buy the first one that meets your basic requirements and move on with your life.
