The 2-Minute Rule: Order in Digital Chaos

Is your "Downloads" folder full of unnamed files? Does your inbox have 1,000 unread emails? You don't need a new app, you need the 2-Minute Rule.

The Basic Principle

This rule, popularized by David Allen (author of GTD), is absurdly simple: If a task takes less than 2 minutes to complete, do it immediately. Don't postpone it. Don't write it down. Do it.

In the digital world, this is magic. Examples:

  • Received an email that only requires an "Ok, got it"? Reply now.
  • Downloaded a PDF? Rename it and move it to its folder now.
  • See a useless notification? Block the app now.

Why It Works (Psychology)

Your brain spends more energy remembering that "you have to do something" (cognitive load) than doing the task itself. Each small pending task is like an open tab in your mental browser. If you close them instantly, your mental RAM stays free for important things.

"External visual chaos creates internal noise. Cleaning your digital environment is cleaning your mind."

How to Start Today

Don't try to fix your whole life in one day. Start with your computer desktop. If you see a file that is useless, delete it. If it's useful, archive it. If it takes less than 2 minutes, do it now.
